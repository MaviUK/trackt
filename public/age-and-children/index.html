Age & Children

Last updated: 13 July 2026

This page explains who may use BURGRS, how age information is used, and what younger users, parents and guardians should know about the service.

Privacy Policy Terms of Use Community Guidelines Age & Children

1. Minimum age

You must be at least 13 years old to create or use a BURGRS account.

BURGRS is not intended for children under 13. A person under 13 must not create an account, even with the permission of a parent or guardian.

If we reasonably believe an account belongs to someone under 13, we may suspend or delete the account and associated personal information.

2. Users aged 13 to 17

Users aged 13 to 17 may use BURGRS, but should do so with the knowledge and permission of a parent or guardian.

A parent or guardian should review the Terms of Use, Privacy Policy and Community Guidelines with the young person.

BURGRS contains public profiles, reviews, replies, comments, chatboards, creator posts, lists and other user-generated content. Some television discussions may involve mature themes, strong language, violence, sexual themes, drug references or other subjects that may not be suitable for every younger user.

3. Date of birth

New users are asked for their date of birth during first-time account setup. We use it to:

  • Confirm that the user meets the minimum age requirement.
  • Identify whether age-appropriate protections may be needed.
  • Support safety, moderation and legal compliance.
  • Keep our app-store age information and service rules accurate.

We do not display a user’s date of birth on their public BURGRS profile. More information about storage and use of personal information is in our Privacy Policy.

4. Public information and online safety

Younger users should never publish:

  • Their home address, school, phone number or private email address.
  • Passwords, login codes, financial details or identity documents.
  • Live location or information showing where they will be at a particular time.
  • Private photographs or messages they would not want shared elsewhere.
  • Personal information about another child without permission.

Public content can be viewed, copied, quoted, linked to or shared by other people. Removing something from BURGRS may not remove copies made outside the service.

5. Contact from other users

Do not respond to anyone who asks for private contact details, sexual images, money, secrecy, an in-person meeting or movement to another messaging service in a way that feels unsafe or inappropriate.

Report the account or content and tell a trusted adult. Serious concerns can also be emailed to info@burgrs.co.uk.

6. Content and behaviour rules

Our Community Guidelines prohibit harassment, hate, threats, child sexual exploitation, grooming, non-consensual sexual material, encouragement of self-harm, illegal activity, impersonation, privacy violations, spam and other harmful behaviour.

We may remove content, restrict features, suspend accounts, permanently close accounts or contact the appropriate authorities where necessary.

7. Advertising and commercial content

BURGRS does not currently use personal information for third-party behavioural advertising.

Where creator content, recommendations or promotions are influenced by payment, gifts or another commercial relationship, that relationship should be clearly disclosed.

8. Parents and guardians

Parents and guardians can help by:

  • Reviewing the young person’s profile and public contributions.
  • Discussing privacy, scams, grooming, spoilers and respectful behaviour.
  • Using device and app-store parental controls where appropriate.
  • Encouraging the young person to report upsetting or unsafe content.
  • Contacting BURGRS if they believe a child’s information is being misused.

9. Underage accounts

To report an account that may belong to a child under 13, email info@burgrs.co.uk with the username or profile link and the reason for the concern.

Do not send unnecessary copies of identity documents. We will ask only for information reasonably needed to investigate or verify a request.

10. Requests concerning a child’s data

A parent, guardian or young person may contact us about access, correction or deletion of personal information. We may need to verify identity, parental responsibility or account ownership before acting.

Requests can be sent to info@burgrs.co.uk.

11. App-store age ratings

Apple and Google assign or display age ratings using their own rating systems and questionnaires. An app-store rating describes the types of content or features that may appear in the app. It does not replace the BURGRS rule that account holders must be at least 13.

12. Changes to this information

We may update this page when BURGRS features, safety controls, legal requirements or app-store rules change. The latest version will be published here with an updated date.
